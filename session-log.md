## 2026-06-22  Phase 6  Build Layer 6 calibration, RPS and backtest
- Did: Implemented calibrate.py, the honesty layer that scores the forecast against real results, with pure scoring separated from backtest orchestration. The metric is the Ranked Probability Score, the right score for ordered three-outcome football, lower is better. rps and mean_rps wrap penaltyblog's rps_array and rps_average, confirmed against the installed 1.11.0 API, rather than hand-rolling. outcome_index and base_rates are pure too, so the gate tests the scorer with no model and no network. run_backtest scores out of sample under strict leakage discipline: it takes the most recent backtest_size matches as a holdout window, fits the goal model once on data strictly before the window start, and asserts training max date is strictly before the holdout start, treating any overlap as a failure. Each holdout match is predicted honouring its actual venue and scored with RPS. A single training cutoff keeps it to one fit because fitting per historical match is expensive, the window is recent and short so the fit is only slightly stale, a conservative bias, and backtest_size is configurable, default 150. The naive baseline is the training base rates applied as a constant on the same matches, computed from training only so both sides are leakage-safe. Added a calibrate block to config.yaml, a wc-calibrate entry point in pipeline.py that prints and logs the model and baseline mean RPS, the matches scored, and the cutoff dates, leaving the forecast flow intact, and artifact.write_calibration_log reusing the provenance pattern. Added tests/test_calibrate_gate.py.

Elo (Layer 2) and squad value (Layer 4) feed the tournament simulator, not the per-match goal model, so a naive leave-one-out toggling them through this backtest shows them at exactly zero delta. That is the architecture, not a harness bug. DC-off is also not a clean leave-one-out, since removing the goal model leaves no per-match probability generator and collapses to the base-rate baseline. Surfaced this before building and the call was to make the ablation a model-versus-model comparison with a new Elo generator, documented openly rather than hidden.

Added the Elo win, draw, loss generator to ratings.py as net-new modeling code: elo_win_draw_loss is the Davidson 1970 ternary extension of the Elo paired comparison, the standard documented way to add draws to a Bradley-Terry or Elo logistic, and at the tie parameter nu equal to zero it collapses exactly to the Elo expected score. fit_draw_parameter sets nu by moment matching the training draw rate, a monotonic bisection on training only, so no leakage. ablation.py scores four configurations on the one shared window: dc_model the reference, elo_model the Elo generator with squad value on, elo_model_minus_squad with it off, and the reused base-rate baseline.
